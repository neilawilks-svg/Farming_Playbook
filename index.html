01 · The Premise

What Farming actually is

A practical approach to helping consultants grow the accounts they are already working in — built on consistent habits, shared language and simple tools that make commercial conversations feel natural rather than forced.

◎

Proactive growth

Account growth within existing engagements — not new logos or cold outreach.

◇

Structured conversations

Client discussions that uncover needs and surface early signals.

◉

Active curiosity

Listening and noticing in day-to-day interactions, not just formal meetings.

✓

Signal capture

Getting opportunities visible and routed to the right account and sales owners.

⚙

Practical enablement

Prompts, tools and examples that make commercial thinking feel usable.

◈

Embedded in delivery

Commercial thinking baked into how engagements run — not a bolt-on.

Farming IS
  • Delivery taking ownership of its role in helping accounts grow
  • Heightened awareness of client needs and signals
  • Commercial curiosity woven into day-to-day delivery
  • Knowing when to deepen, track or route a conversation
  • Brokering the right expertise into the room
Farming IS NOT
  • A separate sales function or process
  • Turning delivery practitioners into salespeople
  • Aggressive selling or chasing revenue targets
  • Pretending to be an expert in everything
  • Working in isolation from account leads and GTM
02 · The Mission

Why this matters

A Delivery-led initiative that helps consultants grow the accounts they are already working in by building consistent habits, confidence and practical tools to spot opportunities, have better client conversations and connect early signals to the right account and sales owners.

It is not about turning Delivery practitioners into salespeople or creating a separate sales process. It is about making commercial curiosity and follow-through a normal part of how we run engagements.

Success looks like

01

More people having the right conversations more often

02

Clearer opportunity signals captured and acted on

03

Stronger collaboration between engagement leads, client partners and account teams

04

Consultants feeling equipped and confident in commercial discussions

03 · Design Principles

Ten principles that shape every session

Click any card to reveal the detail and the playbook implication. These are the rules of the road for facilitators.

01
Delivery credibility comes first
▼

Farming starts with doing the core role well. The first step is to get feet under the desk, establish reputation and deliver credibly before trying to widen the account. This protects the programme from being interpreted as 'sell first' behaviour and keeps it grounded in consultant reality.

Playbook implication

Open with the principle that strong delivery is the licence to farm, not a separate track from it.

02
Farming is heightened awareness, not hard selling
▼

The job is to listen, notice and open the right conversation. The goal is a go-to-market mindset and a sense of where the firm can help — not overt sales behaviour.

Playbook implication

Define Farming as noticing client needs, interpreting them and connecting them to the right next step.

03
Coach from the live account, not generic theory
▼

Sessions are effective when grounded in real accounts, real stakeholders and live constraints. The value comes from helping people interpret what is already happening around them, not from generic training content.

Playbook implication

Every session should be built around one live account and one real growth question.

04
Start with pain points, connect to business goals
▼

Sessions work best when the facilitator helps the consultant move from surface-level project issues to broader account meaning — from project dependencies to business goals and wider objectives.

Playbook implication

Teach consultants to move through a simple ladder — issue, pattern, business impact, client objective, Slalom relevance.

05
Broker expertise, don't pretend to be the expert
▼

The consultant does not need to know everything. The role is often just to unlock the next conversation and bring in the right person from the firm. Bring the right people, not just the nearest people.

Playbook implication

Include explicit language and example scripts for 'I'm not the expert, but I know who should speak to this.'

06
Build around the account lead, not solo heroics
▼

Farming is much more viable when the consultant has an account sponsor or engagement lead to work through. Without one, observations struggle to turn into action.

Playbook implication

Make 'who is your sponsor or account lead?' a mandatory early question and design follow-up around that relationship.

07
Turn observations into trackable opportunity threads
▼

Value is created when consultants move from isolated thoughts to visible, revisited opportunity threads — recurring sessions, line items, progress reviewed every couple of weeks.

Playbook implication

Include a simple opportunity tracker with fields for signal, owner, next action, follow-up date and outcome.

08
Recognise different consultant archetypes
▼

There are at least three Farming starting points: the new-account entrant, the high-performing delivery lead ready for account-level contribution, and the commercially maturing consultant spotting structural opportunities. These are different coaching needs.

Playbook implication

Build the playbook around three pathways — entering a new account, growing influence on an existing account, spotting broader strategic opportunities.

09
Use commercial discussions to deepen delivery
▼

The best sessions are where commercial thinking improves the current engagement rather than distracting from it. Growth emerges from genuine delivery pain — governance, maturity, unused budget — not from pushing new work.

Playbook implication

Show Farming as a way to improve client outcomes first and create growth second.

10
Measure behaviours before revenue
▼

The most useful early signals are leading indicators: account-plan access, recurring growth conversations, stakeholder mapping, cross-capability introductions, problems captured, follow-ups scheduled, credit recorded where work contributes to an opportunity.

Playbook implication

Track behaviours and leading indicators long before any formal sale. Ensure contributions are visible in the system.

04 · Session Flow

The six-step conversation

A lightweight default structure. Relaxed, conversational and anchored in immediate action rather than theory. Use it as a compass — not a script.

1
Why are you here, and what are you trying to get better at?
Set the frame. Understand what the consultant wants out of the session — that shapes everything that follows.
Prompts to try
  • What made you want to have this conversation?
  • What would 'useful' look like by the end?
  • Are you here to explore, plan or act?
2
What is happening on the account right now?
Ground the session in the live account. Get a current, honest picture before going anywhere near opportunity.
Prompts to try
  • Who are the key people you're working with?
  • What's the shape of the work — scope, pace, pressure?
  • Who is the engagement lead or sponsor?
3
Where are the pain points, blockers or recurring frustrations?
Surface the real signals. These are often where the growth conversation quietly lives.
Prompts to try
  • What keeps coming up in conversations?
  • What are people working around rather than solving?
  • Where does delivery feel harder than it should?
4
What wider business goals or plans do those issues connect to?
Climb the ladder. Turn project-level pain into strategic relevance.
Prompts to try
  • What is the client actually trying to achieve?
  • How does this work ladder up to their objectives?
  • Where are the gaps between stated goals and current reality?
5
Who else should be brought into the conversation?
Broker, don't hoard. Identify the right Slalom people and the right client stakeholders.
Prompts to try
  • Who at the firm has the expertise this needs?
  • Who at the client hasn't been in the room yet?
  • Is there a capability introduction that makes sense?
6
What are the next one to three actions, and who owns them?
Leave with momentum. Make commitments visible, small and time-bound.
Prompts to try
  • What's the single most useful thing to do this week?
  • Who owns it, and when will you revisit?
  • Should this thread be tracked, routed or deepened?
05 · Consultant Archetypes

Three starting points, three coaching needs

Not every consultant needs the same conversation. Pick the pathway that matches where they are — and calibrate the session around it.

Pathway 01

New-Account Entrant

Just landed in a new account. Building credibility, mapping the landscape and learning the terrain. May not yet know who the engagement lead is.

Focus the session onGetting feet under the desk first, identifying sponsors, building an early observation lens for the account.
Pathway 02

Existing-Account Grower

Already delivering well and trusted by the client. Wants to move from excellent execution into account-level contribution and visible growth.

Focus the session onAccount plan access, recurring growth conversations, naming threads and turning delivery excellence into broader influence.
Pathway 03

Strategic Opportunity Spotter

Commercially maturing consultant who can already see structural client problems. Needs help turning observations into account-growth motion.

Focus the session onConverting pattern recognition into explicit goals, broker introductions and elevated advisory conversations.
06 · The Toolkit

Four tools to put in a consultant's hands

Keep it simple. Each tool earns its place by being usable inside a normal working week.

▤

Opportunity Thread Log

A lightweight tracker for signals, follow-ups and movement over time. Fields for signal, owner, next action, follow-up date and outcome.

Use it to: turn scattered observations into visible threads
◉

Account Objective Lens

Prompts that help consultants connect project-level issues to the client's wider business goals and strategic direction.

Use it to: climb the ladder from issue → pattern → business impact
◈

Stakeholder Map

Especially useful for accounts with split geographies, hidden buying centres or multiple internal groups making decisions.

Use it to: reveal who else should be in the conversation
◇

Referral Language

Conversation starters and scripts for raising an industry insight or connecting a client to the right expert without over-claiming.

Use it to: broker expertise confidently without pretending to be the expert
07 · Calibration

What "good" looks like after a session

Don't judge success by whether a sale was found. Judge it by whether the consultant left clearer, more confident and with something to do next.

✓
The consultant leaves with a clearer commercial lens on their account
✓
One or more opportunity threads are named and captured
✓
A sponsor or engagement lead is identified (or the gap is acknowledged as the first action)
✓
At least one concrete follow-up action is agreed, with an owner and a timeframe
✓
The consultant knows whether to deepen, track or route the conversation onward
✓
The session felt like a conversation, not a training exercise
08 · Tone

Facilitator mindset

Informal Practical Next-step oriented Relaxed Conversational Anchored in action

These sessions work because they feel like conversations, not coaching clinics. Facilitators should keep the tone light, ground every discussion in the live account and make sure both sides leave with something concrete. If a session feels like theory, it has drifted. Bring it back to the account, the people, the next move.
